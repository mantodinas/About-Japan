new Year
(shogatsu)

this is the most important holiday in Japan. While only January 1 is designated as a national holiday, many businesses remain closed through January 3.

national holiday

❮ ❯

Coming of Age
(seijin no hi)

The coming of age of 20 year old men and women is celebrated on this national holiday.

national holiday

❮ ❯

Beginning of spring
(setsubun)

Setsubun is not a national holiday, but celebrated at shrines and temples nationwide.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

National Foundation Day
(kenkoku kinenbi)

According to the earliest Japanese history records, on this day in the year 660 BC the first Japanese emperor was crowned.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Valentine's Day

In Japan, women give chocolates to men on Valentine's Day. It is not a national holiday.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Emperor's Birthday
(tenno no tanjobi)

The birthday of the current emperor is always a national holiday. If the emperor changes, the national holiday changes to the birthday date of the new emperor.

national holiday

❮ ❯

Doll's Festival
(hina matsuri)

On this day, families with girls wish their daughters a successful and happy life. Dolls are displayed in the house together with peach blossoms.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

White Day

The opposite of Valentine's Day: Men give cakes or chocolates to women. It is not a national holiday.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Spring Equinox Day
(shunbun no hi)

Graves are visited during the week (ohigan) of the Equinox Day.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Showa Day
(Showa no hi)

The birthday of former Emperor Showa Hirohito. Before 2007, April 29 was known as Greenery Day (now celebrated on May 4).

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Constitution Day
(kenpo kinenbi)

A national holiday remembering the new constitution, which was put into effect after the war.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Greenery Day
(midori no hi)

Until 2006, Greenery Day was celebrated on April 29, the former Emperor Showa's birthday, due to the emperor's love for plants and nature.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Children's Day
(kodomo no hi)

Also called boy's festival.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Ocean Day
(umi no hi)

A recently introduced national holiday to celebrate the ocean. The day marks the return of Emperor Meiji from a boat trip to Hokkaido in 1876.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Mountain Day
(yama no hi)

Newly introduced in 2016, this national holiday celebrates mountains.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Obon

Obon is a Buddhist event to commemorate deceased ancestors.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Respect for the Aged Day
(keiro no hi)

This national holiday was established in 1966 as a day to respect the elderly, longevity and celebrate long life.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Autumn Equinox Day
(shubun no hi)

Graves are visited during the week (ohigan) of the Equinox Day. and visiting shrines and temples. People also celebrate the good weather and autumn harvest by enjoying outdoor activities

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Health and Sports Day
(taiiku no hi)

On that day in 1964, the Olympic games of Tokyo were opened.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Culture Day
(bunka no hi)

A day for promotion of culture and the love of freedom and peace. On culture day, schools and the government award selected persons for their special, cultural achievements. The day was originally a public holiday because it was Emperor Meiji's birthday. Later in 1946, the post-war constitution was promulgated on this date which led to the establishment of Culture Day.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Seven-Five-Three
(shichigosan)

Girls of age three and seven and boys of age three and five are celebrated on Shichigosan around November 15, and it is prayed for their good health and growth.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Labor Thanksgiving Day
(kinro kansha no hi)

A national holiday for honoring labor.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

Christmas

Christmas is not a national holiday.

not national holiday

❮ ❯

New Year's Eve
(omisoka)

December 31 is not a national holiday.

not national holiday
